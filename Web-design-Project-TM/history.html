The history of the city of Porto is rich and filled with significant events. Here are six key dates, arranged chronologically, that marked the history of this Portuguese city:

21st Century - Porto as European Best Destination (2012, 2014, 2017)

In the 21st century, Porto received international recognition as a vibrant and attractive destination. It was named the European Best Destination in 2012, 2014, and 2017, drawing attention to its cultural richness, historical significance, and architectural beauty.

20th Century - Carnation Revolution (April 25, 1974)

The Carnation Revolution in Portugal on April 25, 1974, had a profound impact on the nation, including Porto. The revolution led to the establishment of democracy in Portugal, ending decades of authoritarian rule.

19th Century - Industrial Revolution and Dom Luís I Bridge (1886)

The 19th century saw the advent of the Industrial Revolution in Porto, leading to significant industrial growth and trade. In 1886, the completion of the iconic Dom Luís I Bridge further facilitated connectivity between Porto and Vila Nova de Gaia, marking a crucial point in the city's development.

15th Century - Age of Discoveries and Maritime Trade (15th-16th centuries)

Porto played a key role in the Portuguese Age of Discoveries in the 15th and 16th centuries. As a major center for maritime trade, the city became instrumental in Portugal's exploration of new territories, contributing to the expansion of the Portuguese empire.

12th Century - Conquest by the Christians (1147)

In 1147, during the Christian Reconquest, Porto was captured from the Moors by Dom Afonso Henriques, the first king of Portugal. This conquest played a pivotal role in the formation of the Kingdom of Portugal.

Around 300 B.C. - Roman Foundation:

Porto, originally named Portus Cale, was founded by the Romans in the 4th century B.C. This marked the establishment of a settlement that would later contribute significantly to trade and culture.
